# Project Discovery

## Problem

Two systems are failing in parallel:

- People returning from incarceration face severe employment barriers.
- Animal shelters face labor shortages, burnout, and unmet animal-care work.

Project Nova connects these needs through paid, grant-funded transitional employment.

## Key insight

The core intervention is paid work. Animal shelters are the first host-worksite vertical because the work is meaningful, visible, credentialable, and urgently needed.

## Product hypothesis

A single coordinator can manage more participants and shelters when screening, placement, hours, documentation, compliance, and reporting are handled through one auditable platform.

## Early assumptions

- Nova or its fiscal/employer partner is the employer of record.
- Shelters will participate if payroll and employment liability remain outside the shelter.
- Prison dog-program graduates are a strong pilot cohort.
- One grant can fund one placement in MVP.
- A small pilot can validate the operating model.

## Open validation areas

- Jurisdiction-specific screening requirements
- Insurance and workers’ compensation structure
- Payroll provider and employer-of-record partner
- Shelter capacity and supervision commitments
- Grant reporting formats
- Document retention periods

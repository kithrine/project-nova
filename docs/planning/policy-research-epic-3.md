## Research question

What should Nova record before a participant is ready to be matched to an
animal shelter, and what must wait until a particular site and assignment are
known?

## Reentry and transitional-employment models

The Center for Employment Opportunities begins with brief paid orientation and
job-readiness preparation, then continues coaching during transitional work.
Its model includes employment-document help, PPE, workplace communication,
digital and financial skills, mock interviews, resumes, and feedback after
shifts. The evaluated model used a four-day readiness class before transitional
employment.

- CEO, _Our Model_: <https://www.ceoworks.org/our-model>
- MDRC, _Early Impacts of the CEO Prisoner Reentry Program_:
  <https://www.mdrc.org/work/publications/early-impacts-evaluation-center-employment-opportunities-ceo-prisoner-reentry>

The U.S. Department of Labor combines prior-learning assessment, financial and
digital literacy, work-based learning, pre-apprenticeship/apprenticeship,
credentials, and paid work. DOL distinguishes Credential Attainment from
Measurable Skill Gains; a skill gain may be demonstrated through a provider or
employer progress report or a technical benchmark rather than attendance.

- DOL, _Reentry Employment Opportunities_:
  <https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/reentry>
- DOL, _WIOA Performance Indicators_:
  <https://www.dol.gov/agencies/eta/performance/performance-indicators>
- DOL, _Work-Based Learning for Justice-Involved Individuals_:
  <https://www.dol.gov/sites/dolgov/files/ETA/publications/ETAOP_2023-07_Advancing_Employment_Opportunities_for_Justice-Involved_Individuals_through_Work-Based_Learning_Experiences_%28Issue%20Brief%29.pdf>
- DOL, _Implementing Employment Programs to Support Reentry_:
  <https://www.dol.gov/sites/dolgov/files/ETA/publications/ETAOP_2023-05_Implementing_Employment_Programs_to_Support_Reentry_Lessons_from_the_Reentry_Project_Grants.pdf>

**Implication:** keep universal preparation concise and portable, record
evidence of learning, and separate advanced learning and credentials from the
initial readiness gate.

## Animal-shelter standards

The Association of Shelter Veterinarians recommends training for each assigned
task, maintained training documentation, ongoing feedback, and demonstrated
knowledge/skill before proficiency is assumed. Core topics include animal body
language, safe handling, bite prevention, sanitation, zoonotic-risk
recognition, communication, and staff well-being. Duties requiring a license or
credential remain restricted to qualified personnel.

- Association of Shelter Veterinarians, _Guidelines for Standards of Care in
  Animal Shelters_:
  <https://www.sheltervet.org/guidelines-for-standards-of-care-in-animal-shelters>
- Open-access full guidelines:
  <https://www.aspcapro.org/sites/default/files/2023-05/asvguidelinessecondedition-2022.pdf>

CDC/NIOSH recommends a workplace-specific program with documented training
before work and refreshers when needed. Topics include hazards, infection
control, safe handling/restraint, sharps, PPE, injury reporting, and emergency
procedures.

- CDC/NIOSH, _Veterinary Safety and Health — Prevention_:
  <https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/veterinary/prevention/index.html>

ASPCA/Fear Free materials reinforce body-language recognition, objective
observation, minimizing stress, safe handling, disease transmission, cleaning
and disinfection, PPE, and hygiene.

- <https://www.aspcapro.org/resource/fear-free-resources-animal-shelters>
- <https://www.aspcapro.org/resource/online-animal-behavior-training-aspca-learning-lab>
- <https://www.aspcapro.org/resource/sanitation-disease-prevention-animal-shelters>
- <https://university.maddiesfund.org/p/fundamentals>

**Implication:** common behavior, handling, sanitation, and infection-control
foundations fit before matching, but course completion cannot represent
independent proficiency on a host's animals, chemicals, equipment, or SOPs.

## Washington workplace-specific duties

Washington employers must maintain a written Accident Prevention Program
tailored to actual hazards. The Core Rules require safety orientation and
on-the-job orientation for the initial assignment, including injury/first-aid
reporting, unsafe-condition reporting, PPE, emergencies, and exits. Hazard
Communication and PPE duties depend on the site and work.

- Washington L&I, _Accident Prevention Program_:
  <https://www.lni.wa.gov/safety-health/preventing-injuries-illnesses/create-a-safety-program/accident-prevention-program>
- Washington L&I, _Safety and Health Core Rules (WAC 296-800)_:
  <https://www.lni.wa.gov/safety-health/safety-rules/chapter-pdfs/WAC296-800.pdf>
- Washington L&I, _Hazard Communication (WAC 296-901)_:
  <https://www.lni.wa.gov/safety-health/safety-rules/chapter-pdfs/WAC296-901.pdf>
- Washington L&I, _Personal Protective Equipment_:
  <https://www.lni.wa.gov/safety-health/preventing-injuries-illnesses/get-started-with-safety-health/personal-protective-equipment-ppe>
- Washington L&I, _Infectious Disease_:
  <https://lni.wa.gov/safety-health/safety-topics/topics/infectious-disease>

Washington's temporary-worker model is a relevant analogy and may apply
directly depending on Nova's final arrangement. It assigns general safety
training to the staffing agency and site-specific hazard training,
documentation, changed-hazard retraining, and PPE to the host. Federal OSHA
also describes shared responsibility and recommends contracts define duties.

- Washington L&I, _Temporary Workers_:
  <https://www.lni.wa.gov/safety-health/safety-topics/topics/temporary-workers>
- OSHA, _Protecting Temporary Workers_:
  <https://www.osha.gov/temporaryworkers>
- OSHA, _Communication and Coordination_:
  <https://www.osha.gov/safety-management/communication>

**Implication:** Story 3.4 is the portable general layer; Story 5.4 is the
workplace/assignment-specific layer and records host confirmation. Story 3.4
must not be described as satisfying a host's WISHA duties.

## Adopted MVP policy

| Layer                 | Gate                 | Required content                                                                                                                                            | Evidence                                                                           |
| --------------------- | -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Portable pre-matching | Ready for Matching   | Workplace Readiness and Communication; Animal Behavior, Humane Handling, and Bite Prevention Foundations; Shelter Sanitation, Zoonoses, and PPE Foundations | Assessment, provider verification, observed competency, or verified prior learning |
| Host-site onboarding  | Placement activation | Actual hazards, APP/emergency orientation, chemicals/SDS, PPE, SOPs/equipment, restrictions and demonstrated proficiency                                    | Host instruction plus supervisor/host confirmation                                 |
| Credentials           | Requirement-specific | Issuer-backed or expiring credentials                                                                                                                       | Issuer, issue date, expiration where applicable                                    |

The lifecycle, history, visibility, and catalog rules are binding in `ADR-017`.
